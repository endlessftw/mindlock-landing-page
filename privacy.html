Privacy Policy for MindLock

Updated: January 11, 2026

Introduction

MindLock ("we", "our", or "the extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how MindLock collects, uses, and safeguards your information when you use our Chrome browser extension.

Information We Collect

MindLock collects the following types of data to provide its core functionality:

1. Web History

  • What we collect: URLs and page titles of websites you visit
  • Why we collect it: To enforce website blocking rules and generate browsing statistics
  • How it's used: To detect when you visit a blocked site and redirect you to the blocked page

2. User Activity

  • What we collect: Active tab information, time spent on websites, and idle status
  • Why we collect it: To track your browsing time and provide accurate statistics
  • How it's used: To create daily and weekly analytics showing your most-visited websites

3. Website Content

  • What we collect: Website URLs, titles, and favicons
  • Why we collect it: To display website information in your blocked sites list and statistics dashboard
  • How it's used: To show visual representations of your blocked websites and browsing patterns

4. User Preferences

  • What we collect: Blocked website lists, time limits, theme preferences, custom messages, and group settings
  • Why we collect it: To save your configuration and settings
  • How it's used: To maintain your blocking rules and preferences across browser sessions

How We Use Your Information

MindLock uses your information solely for the following purposes:

  • Website Blocking: To detect and block access to websites you've marked as distracting
  • Time Tracking: To monitor and record time spent on different websites
  • Statistics Display: To show you daily and weekly analytics about your browsing habits
  • Time Limits: To enforce daily time limits on specific websites
  • Settings Sync: To save and sync your preferences across devices (via Chrome Sync)

Data Storage and Security

Local Storage

  • All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage API
  • Data is stored in two locations:
    • Chrome Sync Storage: Settings, blocked websites, time limits, and preferences (synced across your devices)
    • Chrome Local Storage: Time tracking data and statistics (stored only on your device)

Data Security

  • No data is transmitted to external servers
  • No data is sold or shared with third parties
  • No data leaves your browser except through Chrome's own sync functionality (if you have Chrome Sync enabled)
  • All processing happens locally on your device

Data Sharing and Third Parties

MindLock does NOT:

  • Sell or transfer your data to third parties
  • Share your browsing history with anyone
  • Send data to external servers or analytics services
  • Use your data for advertising or marketing purposes
  • Track you across websites outside of the extension's functionality

Your Rights and Control

You have complete control over your data:

Access Your Data

  • View all your blocked websites in the extension dashboard
  • See your browsing statistics and time tracking data
  • Review your settings and preferences at any time

Delete Your Data

  • Remove individual websites from your blocked list
  • Clear your time tracking statistics
  • Uninstall the extension to delete all stored data permanently

Modify Your Data

  • Add or remove blocked websites anytime
  • Change time limits and settings
  • Customize your preferences

Chrome Sync

If you have Chrome Sync enabled in your browser:

  • Your blocked websites, time limits, and settings will sync across your devices
  • Time tracking data remains local and does not sync
  • You can disable Chrome Sync at any time in Chrome settings

Data Retention

  • Browsing statistics: Daily statistics are saved as "Yesterday" if exactly 1 day has passed, then reset
  • Weekly statistics: Reset every Sunday at midnight
  • Blocked websites and settings: Retained until you manually remove them or uninstall the extension
  • All data is deleted when you uninstall MindLock

Permissions Explained

MindLock requires the following permissions:

Permission Purpose
storage Save your blocked websites, settings, and time tracking data
tabs Detect when you visit blocked websites and track browsing time
webNavigation Monitor URL changes to block websites in real-time
idle Detect when you're away to pause time tracking
notifications Send alerts when time limits are reached (optional feature)
alarms Schedule automatic data saves and daily/weekly resets
host_permissions (all URLs) Block any website you choose across the entire web

Children's Privacy

MindLock does not knowingly collect information from children under 13. The extension is designed for general use and does not target children specifically. No personal information is collected beyond browsing data necessary for the extension's functionality.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do:

  • The "Last Updated" date at the top will be revised
  • Significant changes will be communicated through the extension's update notes
  • Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy

Open Source and Transparency

MindLock's code is available for review. You can inspect exactly what data is collected and how it's used by reviewing the extension's source code.

Contact Information

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or MindLock's data practices, you can:

  • Contact us through the Chrome Web Store support page
  • Submit feedback through the extension's settings page

Compliance

This Privacy Policy complies with:

  • Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) principles
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) requirements

Your Consent

By installing and using MindLock, you consent to this Privacy Policy and the collection and use of information as described herein.

Summary

In simple terms:

  • MindLock only collects browsing data needed to block websites and show you statistics
  • All data stays on your device
  • Nothing is sent to external servers
  • No third parties have access to your data
  • You can delete everything by uninstalling the extension

MindLock is designed with privacy as a priority. Your browsing data is yours alone.
